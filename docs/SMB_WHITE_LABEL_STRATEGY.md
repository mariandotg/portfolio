# SMB White-Label Strategy to Reach $5k MRR

## Goal
- Build a repeatable SMB business that reaches about `$5,000 MRR`.
- Use one reusable product with tenant setup, minimal personalization, and recurring support.
- Keep custom work as optional paid add-ons, not part of base delivery.

## Executive Verdict
- Your idea is good and growable.
- The strongest version is a **vertical productized-service model**:
- One multi-tenant core product.
- Done-for-you implementation.
- Subscription for hosting, support, and updates.
- Customization upsells with strict scope control.

## Best Business Model
### Model
- **Vertical SaaS + Productized Implementation**.

### Why This Is the Best Fit
- Easier to sell than pure custom development because the promise is outcome-based.
- Better margins over time because delivery becomes template/SOP driven.
- Better cash flow because setup fees fund acquisition and product improvements.
- More stable than project-only freelancing because revenue recurs monthly.

### Non-Negotiables
- Pick one niche first. Do not launch horizontal.
- Keep core product config-driven, not client-code driven.
- Productize repeated requests. Avoid one-off custom branches.
- Define support boundaries and SLAs by plan.

## Offer Design
### Core Offer
- Deploy your product for one SMB in a specific niche.
- Configure tenant setup: branding, domain, workflows, and approved integrations.
- Deliver onboarding and handoff so they can operate confidently.

### Pricing Structure
- `Setup fee`: `$1,500 to $4,000` one-time.
- `Subscription`: `$399 to $899` per month.
- `Customization add-ons`: fixed scope starting at `$1,000+`.

### Suggested Plans
- `Launch ($399/mo)`: hosting, updates, monitoring, email support.
- `Growth ($649/mo)`: Launch + monthly optimization call + faster SLA.
- `Scale ($899/mo)`: Growth + priority support + automation/reporting enhancements.

### Custom Work Policy
- Base subscription includes only small operational tweaks.
- Any larger request is sold as a fixed-scope add-on or enhancement retainer.

## Path to $5k MRR
### Recommended Target Mix
- `8 clients x $649/mo = $5,192 MRR`.
- Setup revenue during ramp: `8 x $2,000 = $16,000` one-time cash.

### Alternate Mix
- `6 clients x $799/mo = $4,794 MRR`.
- Add one small retainer or one additional client to cross `$5k`.

### KPI Targets
- Close `2 new clients/month` during first 90 days.
- Keep monthly churn below `4%`.
- Keep support load below `2 hours/client/month`.

## What to Productize First
### Pick One Painful Workflow
- Lead capture + follow-up + booking + simple CRM for one niche.
- Client portal + reporting + invoicing workflow for one niche.
- Quote-to-payment workflow for one niche.

### Niche Selection Criteria
- Frequent and expensive pain.
- Fast buyer decision cycle (owner/founder/ops lead).
- Similar workflows across businesses.
- Weak existing alternatives or poor implementation in market.

### Good Niches to Evaluate
- Multi-location service businesses.
- Specialty clinics and med spas.
- Professional services firms.
- Local franchises with repeated operational workflows.

## How to Find Clients
### Positioning Statement
- “I help [niche] launch [outcome] in [timeframe] using a prebuilt platform customized to their operations.”

### Channel Priority
- Direct outbound to niche owners/operators.
- Referral partners (agencies, consultants, fractional COOs).
- Short authority content (before/after workflows, mini case studies).
- Warm network and former clients.

### Outbound Machine
- Build first list of `150` qualified targets in one niche.
- Send `10-20` personalized messages daily.
- Book 20-minute fit calls with clear agenda and outcome.
- Use problem-cost language, not stack language.

### Message Angle That Converts
- Current pain and business cost.
- Faster implementation than full custom build.
- Predictable recurring cost.
- Low-risk first phase (setup + first-month success milestone).

### Partner Motion
- Source 10 agencies/consultants already serving your niche.
- Offer white-label fulfillment or referral rev-share.
- Provide partner sheet, demo tenant, and simple pricing.

## 90-Day Execution Plan
### Days 1-14
- Lock niche, core problem, and pricing.
- Build one demo tenant and one niche landing page.
- Finalize onboarding SOP and support SLA.

### Days 15-45
- Run daily outbound.
- Hold `3-5` discovery calls per week.
- Close first 2 paid setups.
- Convert onboarding outcomes into mini case studies.

### Days 46-75
- Refine delivery SOP from real client work.
- Start partner outreach.
- Publish 2 niche-relevant posts weekly.
- Close 2-3 more clients.

### Days 76-90
- Increase outbound volume with tighter ICP filters.
- Improve close rate with stronger ROI framing.
- Offer annual prepay option for cash flow.
- Reach `5-7` active subscriptions and push to `8+`.

## Sales and Delivery Discipline
### Sales Process
- Discovery call.
- Fit/no-fit decision within 24 hours.
- Proposal with setup fee, monthly plan, scope boundaries, and go-live date.
- Kickoff within 7 days of signature.

### Qualification Questions
- What process is broken today?
- What is the monthly cost of this problem?
- Who owns the budget decision?
- When do you need to go live?
- What budget range is approved for setup + ongoing support?

### Delivery Operating Rules
- Use one onboarding checklist for every tenant.
- Keep integrations to an approved list.
- Batch maintenance and support operations weekly.
- Track repeated requests and only productize validated patterns.

## Risks and Mitigations
- Risk: custom requests erode margins.
- Mitigation: strict add-on pricing and scope policy.
- Risk: wrong niche demand.
- Mitigation: complete 30 discovery conversations before heavy build expansion.
- Risk: early churn from weak onboarding.
- Mitigation: first-30-day activation milestones and proactive check-ins.

## Financial Guardrails
- CAC payback: `< 2 months`.
- Subscription gross margin: `> 70%`.
- Monthly churn: `< 4%`.
- Founder support time: `< 20%` of weekly capacity.

## What Success Looks Like at $5k MRR
- 7-10 active subscriptions.
- Predictable delivery workload.
- Strong boundary between product revenue and custom-project revenue.
- Consistent pipeline of at least 3 qualified calls per week.
