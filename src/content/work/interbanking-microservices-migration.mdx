challenge: "Interbanking's core product — a platform for businesses to manage ARCA tax obligations (VEPs) and financial operations — was built as a monolith. As the product grew, the architecture became a bottleneck: deployments were risky, features took longer to ship, and scaling individual services was impossible without scaling everything."
approach: "Joined the backend team to systematically decouple the monolith into independent microservices. Worked on defining service boundaries based on business domains, implementing inter-service communication patterns, and migrating functionality incrementally to avoid downtime. Each service was designed to be independently deployable and scalable."
outcome: "Successfully migrated core platform functionality from monolith to microservices architecture. The new architecture enabled independent deployments per service, reduced deployment risk, and allowed the team to ship features faster. The platform continued serving financial institutions without interruption during the migration."

## What I Did

- Worked as a backend developer on the platform migration team
- Helped define service boundaries and decompose the monolith by business domain
- Implemented microservices for key platform functionality (VEPs, financial operations)
- Ensured zero-downtime migration with incremental rollout strategy
- Contributed to the new version of the product alongside the existing team
